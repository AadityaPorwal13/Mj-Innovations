Edge AI vs Cloud Surveillance: Which Architecture Is Right for Your Site?

One of the most consequential decisions in deploying an AI video surveillance system is where the AI actually runs. Do you process video at the edge — on hardware co-located with your cameras — or do you send feeds to the cloud for centralised analysis? The answer has major implications for latency, privacy, cost, resilience, and scalability.

What Is Edge AI Surveillance?

Edge AI cameras and edge AI processors run video analytics algorithms locally — on hardware physically located at or near your cameras. Instead of sending raw video to a remote server for analysis, the AI model runs on a dedicated GPU or NPU chip co-located with your cameras. Only metadata, alerts, and flagged clips are transmitted over the network.

This architecture is typically implemented either with AI-embedded smart cameras or with dedicated edge compute boxes (like Device X from MJ Innovations) that connect to standard CCTV cameras and run the full AI stack locally.

What Is Cloud AI Surveillance?

Cloud video analytics sends camera streams to remote servers — either a provider's shared infrastructure or your own private cloud tenancy — where AI models analyse the video and return alerts. The AI processing power scales elastically, and there's no local hardware investment required beyond cameras and network equipment.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Latency: Edge AI wins decisively. Local processing achieves detection-to-alert in under 10ms. Cloud processing adds network round-trip time, typically 50–300ms depending on connection quality. In high-stakes security scenarios, this difference can be critical.

Privacy: Edge AI keeps video data on your site — no footage crosses the internet. For organisations handling sensitive environments (government, healthcare, critical infrastructure), this is often a non-negotiable requirement. Cloud systems require careful data processing agreements and robust encryption to meet equivalent privacy standards.

Resilience: Edge AI continues operating during internet outages. Cloud systems fail entirely when connectivity is lost. For remote sites or environments with unreliable connectivity, edge processing is the only viable option.

Cost structure: Edge requires upfront hardware investment; cloud has lower initial cost but higher ongoing subscription fees. For deployments running 3+ years, edge typically delivers lower total cost of ownership for large camera counts.

Scalability: Cloud scales elastically — adding cameras doesn't require hardware procurement. Edge requires additional hardware per site. For rapid, large-scale rollouts, cloud offers faster initial deployment.

When to Choose Edge AI

  • Government, military, or classified environments requiring data sovereignty
  • Remote sites with limited or unreliable internet connectivity
  • Healthcare environments with HIPAA or equivalent data requirements
  • High-security perimeter protection where sub-10ms response is required
  • Industrial environments where network infrastructure is limited

When to Choose Cloud

  • Rapid deployment where hardware procurement timelines are a barrier
  • Proof-of-concept projects before committing to full infrastructure
  • Environments with excellent connectivity and no data sovereignty requirements
  • Organisations wanting to avoid on-site hardware management

The Hybrid Approach

Many enterprise deployments use a hybrid AI surveillance architecture — edge processing for real-time detection and alerting at each site, with cloud synchronisation for centralised management, cross-site analytics, and long-term forensic storage. This delivers the latency and resilience benefits of edge AI alongside the scalability and centralised visibility of cloud platforms. It is generally the recommended approach for organisations with multiple sites and sensitive data requirements.
